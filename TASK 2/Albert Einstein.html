About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics).

Einstein is best known for his formula E = mcÂ², which describes the equivalence of energy (E) and mass (m) and has become synonymous with his name.

His contributions to physics revolutionized our understanding of the universe, space, and time. Einstein's theories laid the foundation for many scientific advancements and continue to inspire and challenge scientists to this day.
